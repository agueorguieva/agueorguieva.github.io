I am a PhD student at the University of Washington Information School advised by Prof. Nicolas Weber. I use empirical and computational methods to 1. evaluate AI in social and political contexts and 2. to determine the effectiveness of legal regulation in mitigating harmful AI behavior or usage. My work in legal regulation provides insight and improvements for state level data privacy and artificial intelligence legislation. My recent academic affilitations include fellowships at the [Stanford RegLab](https://reglab.stanford.edu/) and the [Center for Democracy and Technology](https://cdt.org/).

Before starting my PhD, I received my degrees in Data Science and Legal Studies at UC Berkeley, taught English in Bulgaria via a Fulbright fellowship, and was a research assistant at the [Institute for Computer Science, Artificial Intelligence, and Technology](https://insait.ai/). At Berkeley, I was the team lead for [Human Contexts and Ethics of Data](https://cdss.berkeley.edu/dsus/human-contexts-and-ethics#section-el--0), a [Coding it Forward](https://codingitforward.com/fellowship) fellow, and helped establish the human-rights centered [&lt;AI &amp; Equality&gt;](https://community.aiequalitytoolbox.com/feed) community.

Before all of this, I was a proud attendee of public schools in California's Central Valley. Thanks to the advice and help of others, I've found opportunities and communities I'm passionate about - I'm always happy to chat about any relevant topics or lend a helping hand where possible, just send me an email.
